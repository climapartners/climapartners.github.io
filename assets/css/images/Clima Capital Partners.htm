Our Team

Joumana Asso

Joumana has over 18 years of investment and advisory experience in emerging markets, including ten years executing project and corporate finance deals in the heavy industry and equity investments in clean technology start-ups, and eight years in managing investment in climate related assets at the International Finance Corporation (IFC), World Bank, and the Climate Investment Funds (CIF).

Prior to finding Clima, Joumana managed the overall private sector portfolio of the Climate Investment Funds and managed the Clean Technology Fund’s operations in 2009-2011.

Joumana holds an MBA from HEC and a DESS in Environmental Studies from the University of Montreal. She has work experience in China, India, the Balkans, Africa, and the MENA region.

Rachel Stern

Rachel has over nine years of experience in climate finance, environmental consulting, sustainability, and research. She has an expertise in climate change issues with focus in renewable energy investments on both the local and international level.

Prior to joining Clima, Rachel worked at the World Bank structuring funds to promote private sector investment in renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean technology, and climate adaptation.

Rachel has a background in economics and also spent several years conducting macroeconomic research at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston.

Anastasia Mukhina

Anastasia started her career in the investment management sector and has extensive experience in project management and coordination of complex projects involving multiple stakeholders.

Prior to joining Clima, Anastasia was a strategy and management consultant for five years where she successfully managed and coordinated a wide range of complex and high profile projects involving market strategy development, industry and sector analysis, strategic planning and communications, and organizational transformation for organizations such as NASA and the Federal Aviation Administration.

Anastasia has an MBA from the Georgetown University McDonough School of Business and a BA in Mathematical Economics from Haverford College. She also holds Project Management Professional (PMP) and Lean Six Sigma Green Belt certificates.

Sohee Gu

Sohee works in the intersection of climate finance and international development and has experience building partnerships across the public and private sector and designing investment plans.

Prior to joining Clima, Sohee contributed to the work of the United Nations and the World Bank Group in accelerating investment in climate-smart projects across the energy, agriculture, water, and transport sectors in emerging and frontier markets, especially in West Africa and South Asia. With a background in international security, trade, and environment policy, Sohee has also advised real asset investors in climate-smart investment opportunities.

Sohee has a master's degree from the Johns Hopkins School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS) and work experience in Korea, Thailand, India, Bangladesh, and West Africa.
