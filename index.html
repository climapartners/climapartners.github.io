What we do

We advise, and create economic value for local economies of our partners through robust climate finance solutions, managing risks, and mitigating climate risks. We believe that value comes from thinking sustainably, leveraging local knowledge, and bringing new technologies to create lasting impact to communities.

We understand climate change, technology, markets, finance, and collaboration. We utilize this knowledge to provide technical assistance and practical implementation for the following:


  • We structure Investment Funds, Financing Facilities, and Project Financing


  • We assess and evaluate markets, their risks, and develop tailored strategies for those markets


  • We help provide access to public financing, and provide sectoral focused pragmatic solutions

Team

We leverage decades of investment experience in business and project development with a clear focus on climate. Our team is uniquely focused on collaboration and learning, creating opportunities and synergies to address the complex challenges our clients face in emerging markets.

  • Joumana Asso

    Managing Director

    Joumana has over 18 years of investment and advisory experience in emerging markets, including ten years executing project and corporate finance deals in the heavy industry and equity investments in clean technology start-ups at the International Finance Corporation, and eight years in managing investment funds in climate related assets, such as renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean technology, forestry and climate adaptation, at the World Bank, the Climate Investment Funds, and the private sector.

    Prior to finding Clima Capital, Joumana contributed to setting up the private sector function, and managed the overall private sector portfolio and mandate of the Climate Investment Funds and managed the Clean Technology Fund’s operations in 2009-2011.

    She is currently serving as a Senior advisor to the Green Climate Fund’s Private Sector Facility.

    Joumana holds an MBA from HEC and a DESS in Environmental Studies from the University of Montreal. She has executed transactions and business development in China, India, the Balkans, East Africa, South Africa, and the MENA region.

  • Lawrence McGrath

    Managing Director

    Lawrence has over twenty years of private equity experience in middle market buyouts primarily in infrastructure and clean energy projects, either through direct investments and co-investments or by establishing consortium of well aligned investors.

    Lawrence has acquired and developed green infrastructure projects in central and Western Europe. He also has extensive experience with Western European companies in developing investments for transportation and power generation, solar, wind and run of river hydro projects.

    Lawrence’s career began with the acquisitions of State-owned electric distribution and power companies in Peru, Argentina, Brazil and Bolivia during the 1990’s. As a result, he is familiar with contracted power generation projects and regulated utilities.

    Responsibilities included origination, shareholder agreement negotiations, deal structuring, due diligence, financial analysis, strategic sales and fund management. Member of the investment committee and Board representative for the companies acquired under his leadership.

  • Nicholas Kingston

    Strategy & Investment

    Nicholas has over 12 years of private and public sector experience leading teams through the entire life cycle of change, from vision to execution. His primary area of focus has been in the field of renewable energy and climate change. He worked on complex multi-stakeholder projects designing business models, financial strategies, and data / IT strategies for large consortiums and Multilateral Development Banks. He co-founded and developed the business advising institutions such as the World Bank Group, UNICEF, European Union, and Caribbean Utility providers on Data and Knowledge Strategies that helped improve decisions, investments, and data sharing. Nicholas brings a unique perspective combining his experience in renewable energy infrastructure projects, and strategy advisory, and open data and knowledge initiatives.

    Mr Kingston has an MBA from the American University, Kogod School of Business – Washington DC.

  • Fernando Rivas

    Climate Investments & Advisory

    Fernando has twenty years of extensive energy transactions experience in Latin America. At FE Clean Energy, Mr. Rivas originated a deal flow of projects and developed key relationships in Latin America and Spain, that resulted in a substantive pipeline of investments. His primary role was focused on deal origination, due diligence, structuring, and valuations Prior to joining FE Clean Energy. Fernando was Latin American Business Developer for Petrobras Energia with responsibility for project development in South America and Mexico. Before that, Mr. Rivas was part of the team that managed one of the earliest private equity funds in Argentina, which invested in small family companies in both Argentina and Brazil.

    Fernando graduated from the Belgrano University in Argentina (Licenciado en Administracion) and holds an MBA in Finance from the International Business School of Buenos Aires.

  • Rachel Stern

    Climate Finance Advisory

    Rachel has over nine years of experience in climate finance, environmental consulting, sustainability, and research. She has an expertise in climate change issues with focus in renewable energy investments on both the local and international level.

    Prior to joining Clima, Rachel worked at the World Bank structuring funds to promote private sector investment in renewable energy, energy efficiency, clean technology, and climate adaptation.

    Rachel has a background in economics and also spent several years conducting macroeconomic research at the Federal Reserve Bank of Boston.

    Rachel has an MBA from IE Business School (Madrid, Spain) and is a Fulbright Scholar.

  • Anastasia Mukhina

    Climate & Market Analytics

    Anastasia has extensive experience in program management as well as coordination of complex projects involving multiple stakeholders. She has worked in both private and public sectors, and demonstrated expertise in technology, operations strategy, and program management to enhance efficiency, manage risk, add value, and drive profitability.

    Anastasia started out her career as a business analyst in the technology department for a financial management firm, where over the next 5 years she designed and executed multiple process automation and process re-engineering projects, supporting portfolio accounting, reconciliation, and reporting for the firm as well as its investment affiliates with $47bn to $70bn of assets under management.

    Upon obtaining her MBA in 2011 and prior to joining CCP, Anastasia worked for a strategy and management consulting firm where she managed a number of high-visibility projects in the aerospace industry sector involving program planning, industry and sector analysis, strategic communications, organizational transformation, and development of a strategy for research portfolio management. Anastasia also contributed to these efforts as a subject matter expert in cost-benefit analyses and guided the development of frameworks for economic and technical studies.

    Anastasia has an MBA from the Georgetown University McDonough School of Business and a BA in Mathematical Economics from Haverford College. She also holds Project Management Professional (PMP) and Lean Six Sigma Green Belt certificates.

  • Sohee Gu

    Climate Finance Partnerships

    Sohee works in the intersection of climate finance and international development and has experience building partnerships across the public and private sector and designing investment plans.

    Prior to joining Clima, Sohee worked at the United Nations and the World Bank Group in initiatives that aimed at accelerating investment in climate-smart projects across the energy, agriculture, water, and transport sectors in emerging and frontier markets, especially in West Africa and South Asia. She has background in international security, trade, and environment policy, Sohee has also advised real asset investors in climate-smart investment opportunities.

    Sohee has a master's degree from the Johns Hopkins School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS) and work experience in Korea, Thailand, India, Bangladesh, and West Africa.

  • Kamlesh Pathak

    Climate change policy

    Kamlesh has over 12 years experience in managing projects and programmes in low-emission developments and climate resilient investments with focus on manufacturing, energy infrastructure, and urban and related cross-sectoral issues. This included leadership positions at Asia Carbon Global, a multinational climate change, GHG market and sustainability consulting firm.

    Kamlesh has managed a portfolio of projects for development, registration, issuance and transaction for monetisation of carbon credits under UNFCCC and Voluntary schemes with focus on Kyoto protocol for investment in eligible mitigation sectors. He holds competence in design and project management based on low carbon strategies with focus on climate change mitigation through policies and measures within the UNFCCC framework (CDM, PoA, NAMA, and REDD), (INDCs, NDCs, and GCF), and its Adaptation Private Sector Finance Framework.

    Kamlesh has undertaken design and implementation of environmental and sustainability frameworks for individual and corporate facilities with focus on EHS, Social, Climate and Energy issues, including for Coca Cola Global, Adidas, MoHawk, Delphi, and others. He has advised companies and institutions including auditing, verification and assurance in South Asia and South East Asia.

    Kamlesh holds an MS degree in Environment Management from Forest Research Institute University (FRI University), Dehradun, India and has completed a certificate in ESG and climate change.
